## ⚙️ Configuration Help

Once the software runs, it searches for your relay hardware. Ensure your hardware connects to the same power source as your Wi-Fi router. The setup tool detects your device automatically. If it misses the device, restart your computer and check the physical connections to the relay.

## 🔌 Connecting to Home Assistant

Your relay uses a standard link method to talk to your smart home hub. In the settings screen, look for the integration section. Enter your home hub address and your security credentials. The software handles the rest. Your devices appear in your dashboard after a few seconds.

## 💡 Troubleshooting Tips

*   **Device not found:** Verify the relay power light shows a steady color. Check that your computer uses the same Wi-Fi network as the device.
*   **Slow response:** Move your wireless router closer to the relay or check for signal interference from other household devices.
*   **Page fails to load:** Refresh your browser window. Clear your browser cache if the issue continues.
*   **Language switch issue:** If the language does not change, click the refresh button at the top of the interface screen.
*   **Connection error:** Verify your Wi-Fi password. Incorrect credentials prevent the device from joining your digital network.

## 🏗 Understanding the Hardware

The relay board serves as the bridge between your wall switch and the smart world. It acts as a switch. When you press a button on your phone, the relay moves internally to complete the circuit for your light or fan. This allows you to retain your original wall switches while adding smart capabilities to your home.

## 🛡 Security and Privacy

Your data stays on your local network. The relay communicates directly with your Home Assistant hub or your browser. No external servers track your daily routines or toggle your lights. You control the connection settings manually. Keep your network password safe to ensure only approved devices access these controls.

## 🌐 Managing Multiple Channels

You see four distinct toggle buttons on your dashboard. Use these to turn individual devices on or off. Labels help you remember which device connects to which channel, such as "Kitchen Lights" or "Bedroom Fan." Rename these labels at any time in the settings menu.
